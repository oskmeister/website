Why you shouldn't trust quizzes in social media

by Oskar Werkelin Ahlin, December 30th 2014

Ever seen the results of a quiz with title "What's your IQ?" shared on Facebook? How about "How good is your grammar?"? Usually, they are fake and are designed to just make you share the results, in order to make their site grow as much as possible.

Let's look at a currently very popular example, a popular quiz site from which I see shared results every day: what-character-are-you.com. Here's how a result might look on Facebook:

This really makes you want to take the quiz to see if you can do better than your friend. Your result? Also good! Let's share it. And so it continues.

Well, you've been fooled. Your answers to the questions are irrelevant. On page of the last question, we find the following piece of code that finally will take you to the results page: 

var results = new Array(
    '/d/en/927/result/4875.html',
    '/d/en/927/result/4877.html',
    '/d/en/927/result/4879.html',
    '/d/en/927/result/4881.html');
document.getElementById('answer-form').action = 
    results[Math.floor(Math.random() * results.length)];

Basically, the code populates an array with a bunch of pre-generated score pages (in this case between 12 and 15 points, out of maximum 15) congratulating you about how well the test went for you. As you click one of the alternatives, a dice is thrown and voila, you are redirected to the randomly chosen page. So in this case, you will always get 12, 13, 14 or 15 points, and it does not matter how you answer the questions.

You can try this out by answering the same test with the same answers several times. Or just try to answer as bad as you can and still notice that you get great results.

Now I just wonder how much money they made from the ads on their website. Let's not keep helping silly businesses like this one to grow, shall we?
